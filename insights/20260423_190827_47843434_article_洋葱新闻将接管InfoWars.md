# 洞察报告：The Onion 接管 Infowars

## 基本信息

- **洞察链接**: https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/20/business/infowars-alex-jones-the-onion.html
- **报告日期**: 2026年4月23日
- **主题**: 讽刺新闻网站 The Onion 达成协议接管 Alex Jones 的 Infowars conspiracy 网站

## 事件概述

 satirical news outlet The Onion has reached a deal to take over Alex Jones' Infowars, the conspiracy theorist's far-right media company that has been at the center of years of legal battles. The deal, announced on April 20, 2026, represents a significant development in the ongoing saga surrounding Jones and his media empire.

## 背景

Alex Jones created Infowars with the stated purpose of exposing government secrets, but the platform became notorious for spreading conspiracy theories and misinformation. Most infamously, Jones falsely claimed that the 2012 Sandy Hook Elementary School shooting was a hoax, leading to devastating harassment of the victims' families.

The legal consequences have been severe: Jones has been ordered to pay nearly $1.5 billion in defamation judgments to the families of Sandy Hook victims. He filed for bankruptcy in late 2022, and his parent company, Free Speech Systems, has been facing liquidation.

## 交易详情

The proposed deal includes the following key terms:

- **Licensing Period**: Six months, with an option to renew for another six months
- **Monthly Payment**: $81,000 to cover rent, utilities, and other operating costs for the Infowars studio
- **Court Approval Required**: The deal must be approved by Judge Maya Guerra Gamble in Austin, Texas
- **Future Purchase**: The Onion has also signed an agreement to purchase the full assets once the current judicial stay expires

The deal was negotiated with the court-appointed receiver Gregory Milligan and has the support of the Sandy Hook families.

## The Onion 的计划

The Onion's CEO Ben Collins has outlined ambitious plans for the transformed platform:

- **Creative Direction**: Comedian Tim Heidecker (of Tim & Eric fame) will serve as the creative director
- **Parody Content**: The site will be transformed into a parody of itself, mocking conspiracy theories and misinformation
- **Profit Donation**: Any profits from the new operation will go to the Sandy Hook families
- **Comedy Network**: The goal is to build a larger comedy network around the Infowars platform

Collins described the initiative as "a significant step in an effort to transform one of the internet's more notorious misinformation platforms into a new comedy network for satire."

## Alex Jones 的反应

Jones has vowed to fight the new deal in court. In posts on his personal X account, he claimed The Onion has "fraudulently claimed AGAIN That It Owns Infowars" and accused the company of trying to "steal & misrepresent his identity." He continues broadcasting on his personal social media channels while the legal battle proceeds.

## 法律历程

The road to this deal has been long and complicated:

- **November 2024**: A bankruptcy auction was held where The Onion was named the winning bidder with a $1.75 million offer
- **Early 2025**: A federal bankruptcy judge threw out the auction results, citing problems with the process
- **August 2025**: A Texas state court ruled that Infowars' parent company would be turned over to a court-appointed receiver
- **April 2026**: The new licensing deal is announced, pending court approval

## 专家观点

While the deal has been celebrated by many as a way to neutralize a major misinformation platform, some experts remain skeptical about its long-term impact on the broader misinformation ecosystem. The transformation of Infowars into a parody site may not address the underlying factors that allowed Jones to build his audience.

## 结论

If approved, the deal would represent a remarkable transformation—from one of the internet's most notorious conspiracy hubs to a satirical platform dedicated to mocking the very type of content that made Infowars infamous. The Sandy Hook families, who have pursued years of legal action against Jones, have backed this deal as a way to ensure that "the machinery of lies that Jones built will become a force for social good."
